There are technical and fundamental analysis along with the traders preference for day trading, swing trading, or holding long term. Being that I am a technical analysis swing trader, I rely solely on historical and cyclical data while using certain tools to analyze a stocks entry and exit price. I begin by using a scanner and applying a criteria that includes the tools mention here, MACD, RSI and On-Blance Volume. MACD (Moving Average Convergence Divergence)

MACD stands for Moving Avergage convergence divergence and is one of the more commonly used indicators for technical analysis. This tool follows momentum and indicates whether the stock is headed up or down. This tool uses moving averages and a histogram. As you can see in the picture, when the MACD indicator (in red) crosses the signal line its momentum is headed in that direction. This gives an indication whether it may be a buying opportunity or a selling possibility.

Relative Strenght Index

The RSI, Relative Strenght Index, also tracks momentum and measures the speed and change of a stocks price movements. The indicator ranges from 70 to 30, when the indicator reaches or surpasses 70 the stock is considered overbought indicating it may be time to sell since the stock price is most likely to drop. When the indicator reaches or passes 30, the stock is considered oversold indicating the current stock price may be a good entry point since the price is most likely to go up from there. This tool is very popular is technical traders and give a pretty good idea on what the market is thinking in reagards to buying and selling a particular stock.

On-Balance Volume

On-Balance volume is an indicator of momentum using volume in order to predict price changes. This indicator is a good marker in understanding market sentiment which can predict a bullish or bearish stock. High volume indicates a lot of traffic towards a stock but no way of knowing if market sentiment is bullish or bearish so the tools mentioned here are crucial in using as a relative reference.

Execution/Persistence

Once I have created my table and inputted the stocks I am interested in I can use these indicators to time my buy and sell as best I can. I make sure to create limit sells on each trade to a 2% loss (or what I fell comfortable with depedning on stock, market sentiment, ect), and set a sell stop at the resistance price. Key for success is consitancy and sticking to your plan No matter what!
